 Approach & Learnings

When I first opened the assessment, I realized there were many concepts and terms I wasn’t familiar with.
 Instead of jumping straight into answering, I started by breaking down and understanding each important
 concept individually. I used a combination of AI tools and external web sources, but for more complex 
topics I deliberately relied on official documentation and external sources to ensure accuracy and avoid
 over-reliance on AI.
After building a solid theoretical understanding, I wanted to apply it in practice. I began experimenting
 with setting up a small MCP-like environment locally, with the goal of understanding how an AI could 
interact with tools in a containerized setup. While this was a simplified version, it helped me better 
grasp how MCP servers expose functionality and how an AI agent could use that context to make decisions.
Throughout the process, I was careful not to depend entirely on AI-generated answers. I used AI mainly to
 accelerate understanding, but always validated important points independently.
This assessment taught me several valuable things. It gave me a much clearer picture of how modern
 company infrastructures are structured, which is very different from what I initially assumed. I
 also gained a practical understanding of the MCP concept, including its potential risks such as
 misuse or overexposure of sensitive data. Additionally, I deepened my knowledge of containerization
 and how services can be structured and exposed within such environments.
Overall, this process helped me move from purely theoretical knowledge to
 a more practical and critical understanding of AI-driven infrastructure.
